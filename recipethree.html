Kimchi Fried Rice

History

Kimchi fried rice is a popular fusion dish originating from South Korea. Kimchi is spicy fermented cabbage that's origins can be traced back to 37 BCE-7 AD. At first it wasn't actually spicy, and the introduction to chilli peppers in the 17th century made it like it is today. Rice is a native grain to Asia, and when agriculture was first invented, rice was one of the main crops they farmed. Though the orgins of kimchi fried rice are unclear, it is assumed that it was made with the purpose of being an easy, cheap, low ingrient meal. Kimchi fried rice usually consists of overripe kimchi, leftover rice, green onions, and some type of meat (I usually eat it with pork). An egg is often times added.

Ingredients

  • • 1 Tablespoon cooking oil
  • • 0.5lb sliced pork belly, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • • 1 cup very ripe or overripe kimchi, chopped into bite sized pieces (do not drain the kimchi liquid)
  • • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • • 3 cups cooked rice (preferably cooled down or leftover rice)
  • • 1 green onion, chopped
  • • Seasame oil (to drizzle at the end)
  • •Eggs (fired over easy or sunny side up, 1 egg per serving)

Directions

  1. 1. Heat cooking oil in a large skillet on medium-high heat.
  2. 2. Add pork belly and cook until it's done (no more pink).
  3. 3. Add garlic to the pork belly and stir fry it for 30 seconds (do not let it brown).
  4. 4. Add kimchi and kimchi juices to the pan and stir occasionally, letting the kimchi cook down until it's softened and more translucent.
  5. 5. Add rice to the pan and stir to fully incorporate.
  6. 6. Once rice is heated through and fully coated, add green onions and stir fry for 1 more minute.
  7. 7. Turn off heat and drizzle sesame oil over the rice, then stir to incorporate. Sprinkle sesame seeds over the rice.
  8. 8. To serve, portion the rice into individual bowls and top each with fried egg. Enjoy!
